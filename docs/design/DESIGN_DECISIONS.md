# Design decisions and open questions

The design notes deliberately leave several choices "to be fixed with the
MADMAX team" (Step 1 design, section 26).  This implementation had to pick
concrete defaults to be runnable.  Every default below lives in
`src/madmax_calibration/config.py` and can be changed without touching the
algorithm code.  **Items marked ⚠ need review by the experimental team
before any hardware use.**

## 1. Content and dimension of u_B  ⚠

`u_B = (a_disk[0], a_disk[1], z_global, z_mirror, z_focus)` — two smooth
disk-correction modes (uniform inter-disk gap change; linear gap gradient),
plus global stack translation, reflecting-mirror correction and
focusing-mirror correction.  The disk basis is generated by
`control.disk_mode_basis` and is trivially extended to a third (quadratic)
mode via `ControlConfig.n_disk_modes`.

**Open:** the real correction modes must come from the MADMAX actuator
layout; the mode shapes here are placeholders with the right structure.

## 2. Hard feasibility domain  ⚠

Implemented exactly (never learned): actuator travel box (±0.5 mm disk
modes, ±1 mm global z, ±0.5 mm mirror, ±2 mm focus), minimum physical gap
of 3 mm between adjacent elements, and a maximum single-move step of 0.35
in normalized units (large commanded moves are automatically split into
safe sub-moves by the loop).  **Open:** real CAD limits, piezo strokes,
collision envelopes and cryogenic operating envelopes.

## 3. Scalar objective J  ⚠

Default: `scan_rate` — mean of β⁴ over the target window W (scan rate
scales with β⁴).  `smooth_min` (broadband robustness) and `peak` are also
implemented; the choice is one config field.  Multi-objective/Pareto
calibration (Step 1 design, section 18) is **not** implemented in this
first version; the curve-level data needed to add it later are preserved
in every measurement record.

## 4. Measurement fidelities  ⚠

Two fidelities: the high-fidelity gradient-method boost-factor
determination (treated as an oracle behind `HardwareInterface`) and a
lower-fidelity RF measurement returning power reflectivity |Γ|²(ν) and
group delay τ_g(ν) (roadmap Phase 1.2).  The LF measurement is a
*physics observable the simulator can predict*: it enters the Step-5
joint fit as a second channel y_ℓ = S_ℓ(u, θ) + r_ℓ + ε (design §12,
taken literally), so cheap RF curves constrain θ jointly with HF data.
The affine LF→J link (Step 5 design §12) survives only as the fallback
(`lf_channel = "affine"`) for scalar proxies without a simulator
counterpart.  **Open:** the real reflectivity/group-delay measurement
model and costs, and whether other proxies (coupling) are worth adding.

## 5. Measurement-noise model

Fixed per-point noise from Step-4 uncertainty propagation (shared
normalization + independent per-bin components, Monte-Carlo propagated
through J), cross-checked against baseline replication in Step 0, plus a
jointly inferred noise-inflation term in Step 5.  Full heteroscedastic
modelling is not needed for the mock instrument but the record format
carries per-bin σ(ν) so it can be added.

## 6. Budget model  ⚠

Defaults: 25 HF measurements, 60 LF measurements, 60 booster moves, 60 h
total.  Costs (hours): HF = 1.0, LF = 0.1, antenna alignment = 0.2, move =
0.05 + 0.1 × normalized distance.  All placeholders.

## 7. Soft-constraint risk level

Candidates need a learned feasibility probability ≥ 0.5
(`Step1Config.soft_feasibility_threshold`), and the probability also
multiplies the acquisition.  Only non-damaging failures (measurement
failures at poor coupling) train this model.

## 8. Drift policy

Re-baseline when >12 h have passed since the last baseline/incumbent HF
measurement or when the inferred drift-rate × staleness exceeds 2× the HF
noise.  Step 5 fits a linear drift term in J; the mock detector drifts its
stack offset at 2 µm/h.

## 9. Trust-region policy

Initial half-width 0.25 (normalized), expand ×1.6 after 2 consecutive
validated improvements, shrink ×0.5 after 3 consecutive failures, bounds
[0.02, 0.6], centred on the best validated achieved configuration.

## 10. Simulator stand-in  ⚠

`BoostSimulator` is a 1D transfer-matrix dielectric-haloscope model
(mirror + N disks, axion source term, complex-index losses) with 3 disks
by default (the MADMAX prototype stack) — qualitatively faithful
(transparent-mode boost matches the analytic 1 + 2N(1−1/n²); µm-scale
spacing sensitivity; loss effects) but **not** the real MADMAX
simulation.  The nominal per-window disk spacings and the
booster–antenna distance come from the settings file
(`settings/prototype.toml`); the rest of the package only touches the
simulator through `predict_J` / `beta2`, so the real simulator can be
substituted behind the same interface.

## 11. Step-5 inference level

Level A of the design note: joint MAP over (θ, drift rate, noise
inflation) with the discrepancy GPs marginalized analytically inside the
objective, Laplace uncertainty, prior-sensitivity refit for
identifiability warnings.  The default observation level is
**curve-summary** (design §4.2, roadmap Phase 1.1): each HF measurement
contributes (J, log peak, band centroid, bandwidth, flatness) with one
discrepancy GP per component; `observation_level = "scalar"` retains the
J-only special case for A/B benchmarking.  Level B/C (partial/full
posterior sampling) are future work; the `Step5Result` interface
(posterior samples via `theta_samples`) already matches what they would
provide.

## 12. Step-6 antenna convention

Predictions are for the antenna-aligned booster-level objective F(u_B)
with a plug-in antenna optimum (Step 6 design, section 5, option 1);
antenna-alignment uncertainty is not yet propagated into F.  This is
recorded here as required by the design note.

## 13. θ parameterization  ⚠

Three detector-state parameters: global stack z-offset (correctable via
z_global), uniform inter-disk gap error (correctable via disk mode 0 +
z_global), log loss-scale (diagnostic-only).  The
correctable/diagnostic classification is hard-wired to this control basis
in `DetectorState.CORRECTABLE` and must be revisited whenever the control
basis changes.

## Not implemented (deliberately, per the proposals' staging)

- Multi-objective / Pareto-front calibration (Step 1 §18, Step 6 §10).
- Full curve-level Step-5 inference (§4.3) — the curve-summary level
  (§4.2) is implemented and default; the per-bin curve likelihood is not.
- Full Bayesian sampling (MCMC/HMC) for Step 5 (Level C).
- Knowledge-gradient / entropy-based information terms (the information
  value is the posterior-sd proxy of Step 1 §11).
- Achieved-geometry *distribution* in Step-6 predictions (§6): predictions
  condition on commanded ≈ expected achieved geometry; achieved readback
  is used for all training data.
